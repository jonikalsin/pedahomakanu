The top 5 longest drives in NFL history

The National Football League (NFL) has seen numerous remarkable drives throughout its history, characterized by exceptional skill, strategic play-calling, and sheer determination. From lengthy marches down the field to clutch plays in critical game situations, these drives have left a lasting impact on the sport.

Here, we'll take a look back into the top 5 longest drives in NFL history, showcasing the teams, players, game situations, and notable plays that made these drives truly exceptional.

1. New England Patriots vs. Miami Dolphins - December 29, 2002

Teams: New England Patriots, Miami Dolphins
Players: Tom Brady (Patriots), Troy Brown (Patriots)
Game Situation: Regular season finale, overtime
Drive Details: The Patriots, led by quarterback Tom Brady, orchestrated a masterful 95-yard drive in overtime to secure a 27-24 victory over the Dolphins. The drive showcased Brady's poise under pressure, as he completed several crucial passes, including a game-winning touchdown to Troy Brown. 

2. Indianapolis Colts vs. Green Bay Packers - October 7, 2012

Teams: Indianapolis Colts, Green Bay Packers
Players: Andrew Luck (Colts), Reggie Wayne (Colts)
Game Situation: Late in the fourth quarter, trailing
Drive Details: The Colts, behind rookie sensation Andrew Luck, engineered a remarkable 19-play, 80-yard drive in the final minutes of the game. Facing a daunting deficit against the Packers, Luck displayed remarkable composure as he orchestrated a series of precise throws, culminating in a game-winning touchdown pass to Reggie Wayne.

3. Baltimore Ravens vs. Denver Broncos - January 12, 2013

Teams: Baltimore Ravens, Denver Broncos
Players: Joe Flacco (Ravens), Jacoby Jones (Ravens)
Game Situation: AFC Divisional Playoff, tied in regulation
Drive Details: In a high-stakes playoff matchup, the Ravens, led by quarterback Joe Flacco, engineered a breathtaking 88-yard drive in the waning moments of the fourth quarter. With the game tied, Flacco connected with Jacoby Jones for a game-tying 70-yard touchdown pass, forcing overtime. 

4. Cleveland Browns vs. Kansas City Chiefs - January 17, 2021

Teams: Cleveland Browns, Kansas City Chiefs
Players: Baker Mayfield (Browns), Rashard Higgins (Browns)
Game Situation: AFC Divisional Playoff, trailing in the fourth quarter
Drive Details: In a fiercely contested playoff battle, the Browns, led by quarterback Baker Mayfield, mounted an 18-play, 75-yard drive against the defending champion Chiefs. Despite facing a formidable deficit, Mayfield displayed remarkable composure and precision, culminating in a touchdown pass to Rashard Higgins. 

5. San Francisco 49ers vs. New York Giants - November 28, 1993

Teams: San Francisco 49ers, New York Giants
Players: Steve Young (49ers), Jerry Rice (49ers)
Game Situation: Regular season matchup, trailing in the fourth quarter
Drive Details: In a thrilling regular-season clash, the 49ers, behind the leadership of quarterback Steve Young, engineered a mesmerizing 92-yard drive against the Giants. Young's precision passing and a key reception by Jerry Rice set the stage for a game-winning touchdown, cementing the 49ers' reputation as a dominant force in the NFL.

 

These top 5 longest drives in NFL history stand as testaments to the skill, resilience, and determination of the players and teams involved. From regular-season showdowns to high-stakes playoff battles, these drives have etched their place in NFL lore, leaving an indelible mark on the sport's rich tapestry.
