The last NFL commissioner was Roger Goodell, who assumed office on September 1, 2006. Goodell became the eighth commissioner in the history of the National Football League (NFL). Before him, the league's chief executive was called the league president. The first head of the league was Ralph Hay, the owner of the Canton Bulldogs. Jim Thorpe, a renowned athlete, served as the inaugural president of the league.
